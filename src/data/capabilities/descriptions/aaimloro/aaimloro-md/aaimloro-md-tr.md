## Capability Overview
Analytical AI encompasses the use of machine learning (ML) and operations research (OR) techniques to analyze data, identify patterns, and generate actionable insights. This capability addresses the challenge of extracting value from large, complex datasets to support decision-making, forecasting, optimization, and automation. It enables enterprises to move beyond descriptive analytics toward predictive and prescriptive outcomes, improving operational efficiency and strategic planning. Analytical AI is foundational for embedding intelligence into business processes and systems.

## Business Value & Supported Use Cases
- Enables data-driven decision-making by providing predictive models and optimization solutions.
- Supports use cases such as demand forecasting, customer segmentation, fraud detection, supply chain optimization, and risk assessment.
- Facilitates automation of routine tasks through intelligent process optimization.
- Relevant across AI adoption stages:
  - Experimentation: prototyping models and validating hypotheses.
  - Scale-out: expanding model deployment across multiple business units.
  - Industrialization: integrating models into core business workflows.
  - Regulated production: ensuring compliance and robustness in critical environments.

## Maturity Expectations
- Emerging: Initial pilots with limited scope, manual model management, and inconsistent results.
- Well-established: Repeatable model development lifecycle, standardized data pipelines, and monitored model performance.
- Strategic / Differentiating: Enterprise-wide integration of analytical AI with automated retraining, real-time decision support, and measurable business impact.
- Foundational: Embedded as a core capability with governance, scalability, and resilience ensuring continuous value delivery.
- “Good” at scale means consistent model accuracy, low latency inference, automated lifecycle management, and alignment with business objectives.
- Under-maturity signs include siloed models, lack of monitoring, poor data quality, and inability to operationalize insights.

## Mandatory vs Optional Usage
- Mandatory when:
  - Analytical AI supports production-critical decision processes.
  - Deployed at enterprise scale requiring reliability and governance.
  - Used in regulated industries demanding auditability and compliance.
- Optional when:
  - Limited to proof-of-concept projects or isolated teams exploring feasibility.
  - Applied in non-critical scenarios where failure has minimal impact.
- The classification reflects the need for robustness, traceability, and operational discipline proportional to business risk and scale.

## Key Dependencies & Related Capabilities
- Technical:
  - Data ingestion and management platforms.
  - Feature engineering and data preprocessing pipelines.
  - Model training infrastructure and deployment environments.
- Governance:
  - AI model risk management and validation frameworks.
  - Data privacy and security controls.
- Operational:
  - Monitoring and alerting systems for model performance.
  - MLOps practices for continuous integration and delivery.
- Related capabilities:
  - Generative AI (for synthetic data augmentation).
  - Platform Operations & MLOps / LLMOps.
  - Governance, Risk & Compliance.

## Risks of Omission or Poor Implementation
- Architectural risks: Fragmented or inconsistent model deployment leading to unreliable insights.
- Operational risks: Model drift, lack of monitoring, and failure to update models causing degraded performance.
- Compliance risks: Non-compliance with data governance and audit requirements, especially in regulated sectors.
- Typical failures include siloed analytics efforts, poor data quality, absence of lifecycle management, and inability to scale beyond pilots.

## Example Metrics & KPIs
- Model accuracy, precision, recall, or other relevant performance indicators.
- Percentage of analytical models deployed to production versus in development.
- Mean time to detect and remediate model performance degradation.
- Data pipeline latency and throughput supporting analytical workloads.
- Compliance audit pass rates related to model governance.
- Business impact metrics such as cost savings, revenue uplift, or process efficiency improvements attributable to analytical AI.
